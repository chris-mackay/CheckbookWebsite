Welcome to Checkbook Help

This section contains topics for the main features Checkbook offers and how to use them.

  • My Checkbook Ledgers: Allows you to manage all your ledgers from one place.
  • New Transaction & Edit Transaction: Create new transactions and edit existing ones.
  • Edit Multiple Transactions: Update a bunch of transactions with the same information.
  • Type Codes: Allows you to specify what kind of transaction you are creating.
  • Account Details: See all the important high level information regarding your checking account.
  • Categories & Payees: Specify what types of items you buy and who your money went to or came from.
  • Import Categories & Import Payees: Load categories and payees from your other ledgers into your currently opened ledger.
  • Spending Overview: See detailed information about how you spend and make money.
    • Create hypothetical scenarios with Create New Scenario to help with financial planning.
  • Spending Overview Charts: See your monthly totals from the Category/Payee table in Spending Overview displayed in a color coded chart.
  • Print Chart: Print your Spending Overview Chart to a file.
  • Monthly Income: See a quick display of how you are saving money each month of the year.
  • Budgets: Allows you to keep track of how much you are spending on certain categories and set a limit on your spending.
  • Most Used Categories/Payees: See total occurrences, payments, and deposits for each category and payee in your ledger. Calculate based on year, or your entire ledger.
  • Quick Filter: Find transactions using a search box.
  • Advanced Filter: Find transactions using specific parameters.
  • Options: Personalize the appearance of your ledger. Change colors and grid settings. Choose default directories for quickly accessing files related to Checkbook.
  • Customize Toolbar: Allows you to add, remove, and sort button icons on the toolbar.
  • Import Transactions: Import transactions from a CSV (Comma delimited) (*.csv) file to get a head start on tracking.
  • Balance Account: Keep track of the transactions that have been cleared by your bank.
  • Loan Calculator: A tool for calculating simple interest and compound interest loan amounts.
  • My Statements: Save your monthly bank statements and attach them to transactions for quick viewing.
